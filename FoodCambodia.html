Cambodian Food

Food Varieties

The staple food in Cambodia is rice. You will see rice with every Cambodian meal you order.

Fish Amok

Fish Amok is one of the national dishes in Cambodia. It has been a special dish served in the Royal Palace during the Khmer Empire. It is prepared using a steaming technique (Wikipedia).

Ingredients
- Goby fish/snakehead fish/catfish
- Yellow paste of galanga, kaffir leaves, tumeric and other herbs
- Coconut Cream
- Eggs

KorKo Soup

Korko Soup (Samlor Korko) is another national dish of Cambodia. Korko means 'to stir' and Samlor refers to 'soup' (tasteatlas.com).

Ingredients
- Fish/Chicken/Pork
- Green paste and herbs
- fermented fish (Prahok)
- Coconut Cream
- Pumpkin
- Green banana
- Papaya
- Eggplant
- Long beans
- Chili or other green leaves
- Roasted ground rice

Kuy Teav

Kuy Teav (Noodle Soup) is a Cambodian-Chinese style breakfast dish. It is very popular in the city or township areas. You can see people selling Kuy Teav in wet market, mall, restuarants and on the street.

- Ingredients
- Soup
- Rice noodle
- Chicken or pork or beef or seafoods
- Bean Sprouts

Num Banhchok

Num Banhchok (Cambodian Noodle Soup/gravy) is a Cambodian style breakfast dish. It is very popular in all areas, but more village style. You can see people selling Num Banhchok by hawkers mostlya lady. You can also find it at some food courts and restuarant as well; However, not all food courts and restuarants sell Num Banhchok.

- Ingredients
- Yellow-paste Soup/gravy
- Rice noodle (Num Banhchok
- Steamed ground fish
- Fresh banana flower
- Lily stem
- Fresh long beans
- Fresh cucumbar
- Herbs

Peper Crab

Stir-fried peper crab is a signature dish from coastal area. The two main ingredients are crabs and the green pepercorn from Kampot province that make the dish special.

Ingredients
- Crab
- Green Pepercorn
- Garlic
- Sprin Onions

Street Food

One of the fun activities that tourists love is exploring street food. There are many yummy food and snacks you can find people sell on the street.

Fresh Drinks
- Sugarcane Juice
- Coconut Water
- Fruit Smoothies
Cakes
- Palm Sugar Rice Cake
- Cambodian Donuts
- Banana Fritters

Palm Sugar Rice Cake

Palm Sugar Rice Cake is made of rice and sticky rice flours, filling with a palm sugar. It is topped with young coconut shredding. The topping is important. If ripe coconut shredding is used for the topping, the cake is not yummy.

This cake is also known as 'Husband Killer' cake. Hilarous, isn't it? It is given with this nickname because you can choke if you eat it when it is hot. The heat can be fatal.

Cambodian Donuts (Num Kong)

Cambodian Donuts are called as Num Kong. Num means Cake, and Kong means bracelet. You will see Cambodian Donuts being sold on the street, in the mall and by hawkers. What makes Cambodian Donuts different from western donut is that Cambodian donuts are glazed with palm sugar and sesame seeds.

Cakes sold with Cambodian Donuts:
- Num Kroch
- Num Pong Ansorng

Snacks

Now, a surpise! You may be surprised when you explore street food in Cambodia as you will come across with many types of fried insects.

A majority of Cambodians in rural area like eating fried edible insects as snacks or when they drink. It is considered as one of the delegacy snack in rural areas.

Fried Insects you may see:
- Fried spider
- Fried crickets
- Fried grasshoppers/locusts
